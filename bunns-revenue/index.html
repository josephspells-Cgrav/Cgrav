Every figure in this deck has a source, an assumption, and a sensitivity. Here's where each number comes from, what's included, what isn't, and how a skeptical buyer can verify the math — before signing anything.

Where the $4,800 blended ticket comes from

Almost every revenue number in this deck is calculated as jobs × 12 × $4,800. The $4,800 blended ticket is built from real NC HVAC market pricing:

Job type% of mixAvg ticketContribution

Repair tickets in NC run $95–$2,850 with a $333 state-wide average. Full system installs run $5,000–$12,000, with the $11,400 midpoint reflecting Bunn's positioning (multi-system installs, premium brands, financing options). The 60/40 mix is industry-standard for an established residential HVAC contractor with a full-service offering.

Sensitivity: if the real mix is 70/30 repair-to-install (more service calls than replacements), blended ticket drops to ~$3,700 and all revenue numbers fall ~23%. If it's 50/50, the ticket rises to ~$5,900 and revenue rises ~23%. The current model sits in the middle.

Run rate vs. Year-1 cash — the most important distinction in this deck

This is where most marketing audits quietly mislead. The "Year 1 revenue" you see in most decks is the month-12 run rate annualized, not the actual cash you collect over 12 months. They're different numbers because SEO doesn't work in month 1.

Here's the real SEO ramp curve, supported by industry data from LocalMighty, SearchEngineLand, and our own client cohorts:

PeriodWhat's happening% of run rate
Months 1–3Infrastructure indexing. Google crawls new pages, slow to trust new content. Minimal visible movement.~5%
Months 4–6First ranks appear in low-competition cities. Early leads start trickling in. Reviews velocity ramps.~35%
Months 7–9Authority compounds. Map pack visibility grows. AI citations begin if review threshold crosses.~75%
Months 10–12Steady state reached. This is the "run rate" the deck shows.~100%
Year 1 blended cash ≈ 54% of month-12 run rate~54%

That's why every Year-1 card in this deck now shows both numbers: the annualized run rate (what you're producing by December) and the Year-1 actual cash (what you collected over the full 12 months). They're both real — they just measure different things.

Rocky Mount specifically ramps even slower — about 35% of run rate in Year 1 — because a cold-start market needs to verify a brand-new GMB (mail-delayed postcard verification), hire local technicians, and accumulate reviews from scratch. The Louisburg side of the $5K program runs at the standard 55% ramp because the brand foundation is already there.

How we estimate per-city search volume

Both the Louisburg market (1,460 monthly searches) and the Rocky Mount market (1,300 monthly searches) come from a methodology you can replicate:

  • Population-weighted base estimate: roughly 5–8 monthly HVAC-intent searches per 100 residents
  • Plus a city-specific base rate for emergency / replacement queries (skews higher in older housing stock)
  • Cross-validated against third-party SEO tools (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Google Keyword Planner) for each target keyword in each city
  • Discounted for cities that bleed into highly-competitive metros (Wake Forest is in our 30-mile radius but capture is modeled conservatively because of Triangle-metro spillover)

The breakdown for both markets is itemized in the city tables on Page 01 and Page 03. Every city volume in those tables is defensible against third-party data — and we re-verify them quarterly.

Sensitivity: if real search volume is 30% lower than estimated, traffic capture drops 30% and revenue drops proportionally. If 30% higher, the inverse.

The capture rates and why they grow year over year

Each year card shows "X% capture · Y% lead conv · Z% close." The capture rate grows from Year 1 to Year 3 because five things compound:

  1. Domain age compounds. Google trusts older, consistently-maintained sites more. A 12-month-old site outperforms a 1-month-old site at the same on-page quality.
  2. Backlink profile grows. Each new referring domain adds authority. Authority growth is roughly linear with monthly link velocity.
  3. Reviews accumulate and feed back into rankings. Each completed job → 1 new review. Reviews lift map-pack rank, which lifts click-through, which lifts more reviews. Flywheel.
  4. AIO citations begin once the review threshold crosses ~100. ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini start naming Bunn's when the trust signals stabilize. Typically months 8–14.
  5. Brand search rises. People who see Bunn's once and search again later. This is "dark traffic" that doesn't show up in keyword tools but adds 5–15% to volume by Year 3.

That's why 16% capture in Rocky Mount Year 1 is realistic — at $3K/mo, you're buying aggressive citation velocity from day one, and Rocky Mount's less-entrenched competitive set means there's room to climb fast.

"How do I know these numbers are even accurate?"

Six honest verification mechanisms:

  1. Source-checked baseline data. Search volumes, ticket sizes, demographic data, and competitive review counts all come from publicly verifiable sources (US Census, NC HVAC market pricing surveys, BBB profiles, Yelp/Houzz/Angi review platforms, industry SEO tools like Ahrefs and SEMrush). Every number can be re-derived.
  2. Sensitivity analysis built in. Conservative and optimistic bands carry ±20–35% margin of error documented in the long-form audit. AI search projections carry ±40–50% because the space is moving monthly. The deck uses conservative or mid-range figures, not optimistic ones.
  3. Monthly reporting tracks projection vs. reality. Call tracking (CallRail with unique numbers per city page), GA4, Google Search Console rankings, Google Business Profile insights, review velocity by city — all measured and reported monthly. You'll know within 90 days if reality is matching the model.
  4. Industry benchmark cross-references. The ranges in this deck sit inside published benchmarks from LocalMighty, FirstPageSage, Mediagistic, the Princeton GEO study, Yelp data, Google's own AI Overviews stats, and NEXSTAR. None of the projections require believing in outlier outcomes.
  5. 6-month checkpoint clause. If by month 6 the leading indicators (rankings, reviews, traffic, conversion rate) are off projection by more than 25%, we re-calibrate the model and the strategy. No locked-in 12-month commitment without measurement-based feedback.
  6. The numbers are deliberately conservative. The long-form audit's optimistic-case projections are significantly higher than what's shown in this deck. We chose to underpromise here — if anything, this deck under-states the realistic ceiling.
