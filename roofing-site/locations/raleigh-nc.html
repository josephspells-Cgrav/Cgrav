Frequently Asked Questions

Raleigh Roofing FAQ

Common questions from Raleigh homeowners about roofing costs, insurance, and timelines.

A typical roof replacement in Raleigh ranges from $8,000 to $25,000 depending on roof size, pitch, material choice, and the extent of decking repairs needed. Architectural shingles average $4.50–$6.50 per square foot installed. We provide free detailed estimates so you know the exact cost before any work begins.

Absolutely. We handle the entire insurance claim process for Raleigh homeowners — from the initial inspection to documenting damage to meeting with your adjuster. Our team has recovered over $12 million in insurance claims for Wake County homeowners.

Most residential roof replacements in Raleigh are completed in 1–2 days. Larger or more complex roofs (multi-story, steep pitch, or extensive decking repair) may take 2–3 days. We always provide a timeline before starting.

Raleigh's hot summers, occasional hailstorms, and hurricane-season winds make impact-resistant architectural shingles the most popular choice. We also install metal roofing (excellent for heat reflection) and slate for historic homes in areas like Oakwood and Boylan Heights.

Yes. The City of Raleigh requires a building permit for roof replacements. As a licensed general contractor, Summit Roofing handles all permitting for you — it's included in our service at no extra charge.
