Why MCTB + Opus 4.7 chatbot matter so much

Small home-service businesses miss 20–40% of inbound calls during busy periods (industry data). HVAC callers are in panic mode — broken AC in summer, no heat in winter. If they hit voicemail, they're gone in under two minutes. Missed-call text-back recovers 35–50% of those callers. An always-on Opus 4.7 chatbot does the actual booking — not just engagement. The combined effect is +30–60% on call-to-job conversion, independent of traffic.

09 · AI Search Optimization (AEO / GEO)

The layer most audits miss — and it's compounding fast.

45% of consumers now use AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, Copilot) to find local services. Yet ChatGPT recommends only 1.2% of local businesses, and an industry survey put 87% of HVAC and plumbing contractors in the AI-invisible bucket. Bunn's is squarely in that 87%. This section quantifies the layer on top of the rebuild and off-page work — and unlike most "AI hype" sections in a marketing audit, this one has numbers behind it.

What we build into the site to capture this

On-site (included in rebuild): LocalBusiness schema (with HVACBusiness subtype), Service schema per service page, FAQPage schema, Review schema, llms.txt at root with curated page summaries, explicit allow for GPTBot/ClaudeBot/PerplexityBot/Google-Extended in robots.txt, FAQ content modules in natural-language Q&A format, expert quote callouts (founder, 43-year history, A+ BBB), local statistics blocks (Franklin County climate, equipment lifespan), and clean semantic HTML the LLMs can parse without JS execution.

Why this layer is undervalued by most agencies: AEO measurement tooling is still immature (the analytics layer is 12–18 months behind actual user behavior). Most marketers can't see AI referrals cleanly in GA4, so they undersell it. The 4.4× conversion multiplier is the real story — and it's the lever that turns "AI is small" into "AI is the highest-margin channel you have."
10 · Self-Audit & Model Caveats

What this model handles well — and what it doesn't.

Auditing the auditor. Below is an honest pass at where this model is robust, where it's intentionally bounded, and where the assumptions could miss in either direction.

What the model handles well

  • Service-area realism: explicit Tier 1–4 breakdown by drive time, not theoretical radius
  • Competitive density quantified (Wake Forest top-8 aggregate vs. Bunn's 13 reviews)
  • Conservative / Optimistic banding on every revenue figure
  • Six modeled downside failure modes with independent probabilities (§12)
  • Unit economics traceability — every revenue number traces to ticket size × close rate × volume
  • Capacity sanity check (job volume vs. 12-employee headcount)
  • LTV pipeline shown separately from immediate revenue
  • AEO/GEO offensive + defensive layers (§09)
  • Honest "this number requires hiring" callouts where applicable

What's intentionally out of scope

Channel / topicWhy excluded
Local Services Ads (LSA)Separate paid budget conversation; would add a meaningful incremental layer (~$3K–$8K/mo spend → ~10–20 calls/mo) but isn't part of the SEO scope
Google Ads PPC / Meta AdsPaid channels with different unit economics; would model separately
Email / SMS retention marketingCaptured partially in LTV; not modeled as a discrete uplift
Commercial HVACBunn's positioning is residential-focused per their own copy
Maintenance plan subscriptions (membership model)Partially captured in LTV multiplier; not modeled as a separate ARR stream

Where the model could understate (upside risk)

  • Brand search lift from AI citations. Once Bunn's is named in ChatGPT/Perplexity answers, branded search volume rises — those visitors convert at ~25–40% vs. ~8% non-brand. Compounding effect not fully captured.
  • Seasonal demand spikes. HVAC demand swings +25–40% in June–August and December–February. Model uses steady-state averages.
  • Repeat customer acceleration. LTV is captured but the timing of repeat revenue (year 2–3 follow-up service) isn't laid into the 12-month curve.
  • Word-of-mouth velocity. Each new happy customer generates ~1.4 organic referrals in HVAC (industry data). Not modeled.
  • GMB review compounding past month 12. Year 2 review velocity accelerates as base grows — not in 12-month figures.

Where the model could overstate (downside risk)

  • Tier 3 (Wake Forest) map-pack penetration. Google's local pack is geo-proximity weighted. Bunn's GMB is in Louisburg; they will not consistently appear in the Wake Forest map pack for searchers physically in Wake Forest unless a second GMB-verified service address is established. Tier 3 traffic in the model assumes organic capture via service-area pages, not map pack share.
  • Optimistic capacity assumptions. The optimistic case at $1K/$2K tiers assumes Bunn's hires 3–5 technicians during year one. Real-world HVAC tech hiring takes 2–4 months per role and the labor market is tight.
  • AI search volatility. AEO numbers carry ±40–50% variance vs. ±20–35% for organic. The space is moving monthly.
  • Sustained review velocity. 1 review per job assumes operator follow-through (text-after-job automation). If not implemented rigorously, review velocity halves.
  • Tier 4 (Raleigh) is excluded from this audit — anyone modeling Raleigh capture for a Louisburg-based 12-person shop is fantasizing.

Geo-restriction caveat — re-stating it

Bunn's will not dominate the Wake Forest map pack from Louisburg. The local pack is geo-proximity weighted. Bunn's appears in Wake Forest map pack only when the searcher is physically in Wake Forest and Bunn's has strong proximity-adjacent signals (reviews from Wake Forest customers, citations from Wake Forest publications). Tier 3 revenue in the model relies on organic capture via service-area pages — not map pack dominance. Map pack dominance in Wake Forest requires either (a) a second physical service address there, or (b) ~18+ months of citation building with Wake Forest customer reviews.

What's driving the error bars

Conservative numbers (±15–25%) are tight because they assume only Tier 1 + 2 market capture, modest conversion lift, and no capacity expansion. The variance here is mostly seasonal (HVAC demand swings ±20% summer-to-shoulder).

Optimistic numbers (±25–35%) widen as you go up the off-page tiers because they require Wake Forest market penetration (Tier 3), and Wake Forest is the fiercest competitive zone in Bunn's reachable radius. The $2K/mo tier carries the highest variance because it assumes both review velocity sustaining 18+ months and Bunn's hiring 3–5 technicians on a timeline that's realistic but not guaranteed.

AEO bands (±35–50%) are wider than the rest because the AI search landscape is moving monthly, reporting tooling is immature, and local-services AI traffic share is still a small enough number that small absolute deltas produce large percentage swings. What to actually sell, in order

1. The rebuild bundle is the foundation — and it already includes AEO. Without the 9.5/10 UI/UX + on-page + technical SEO + MCTB + Opus 4.7 chatbot + schema + llms.txt + FAQ structure, no amount of off-page spend converts efficiently. Phone-only intake caps the entire opportunity. AEO is largely an on-site implementation question — schema, structured content, llms.txt — so it's bundled into the rebuild at no incremental cost.

2. Recommend $1,000/mo off-page as the growth tier. The marginal jump from $500 → $1,000 is the single steepest ROI step in the ladder because GMB automation switches on and citation velocity hits the threshold AI platforms require to start naming Bunn's in answers. For a Tier 1/2-focused business like Bunn's, the map pack is the dominant lead source and AI citations are the second-largest unlocked opportunity.

3. Hold $2,000/mo for year two, once capacity and Tier 3 (Wake Forest) penetration are proven. Pitching $2K/mo before Bunn's has hired 3–5 additional technicians is selling more leads than they can serve, which damages the review flywheel and the AI citation credibility (AI deprioritizes brands with declining review trends).

4. $500/mo is the budget-constrained close path only if the client can't commit to $1K/mo. Still positive ROI, but cuts the AEO citation velocity in half — Bunn's may not cross the AI-trusted review threshold within 12 months on this tier.
