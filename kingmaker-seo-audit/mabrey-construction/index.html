A No Profile Exists — the Map Has No Mabrey Entry

We searched Google's business index at 40 km around Durham: no Mabrey Construction listing exists. On Google Maps — the surface where this category's calls happen — there is no entry to rank, at any position. The live scan in Part 4 follows from this single fact: a grid can only rank listings that exist.

The move: create and verify a Business Profile for Mabrey Construction. The anchors are already in place — a real shared office at 519 Valleymede Dr and the construction arm's own phone line, (984) 464-4188.

B The Construction Work Rides the Roofing Listing

Google has attached construction services — "Custom Home Construction," "New Home Construction Services," "Porch & Deck Construction" — to a listing categorized Roofing contractor. Google distributes relevance across the categories present — both trades currently share one roofing-shaped signal.

The mechanism: the primary category is the strongest single field on a profile. A dedicated Mabrey Construction profile — primary category "Custom home builder" or "General contractor" — gives the construction work its own category signal and lets the roofing listing stay concentrated on roofing.

C The Map Field Is Open

The live 49-point scan in Part 4 measured every competitor on this grid. The strongest — BOLD Construction & Renovations — holds 26.53% of the grid with 44 reviews. No one owns this map.

The counter-example sits in the same data: CQC Home carries 290 reviews and appears in 0% of this grid. The primary category, not review mass, decides eligibility here.

What that means for a new profile: the measured ceiling in this category is a quarter of the grid, held at 44 reviews. A correctly categorized profile enters a field where the leader's position is defined by numbers a new entrant can actually reach.

D The Review Gap — Quarters, Not Years

Pack-leading construction competitors on this grid hold 27–64 reviews. At a steady 10–15 reviews a month, a new profile crosses the measured leader's 44 inside three quarters.

The move: a same-day review ask on every completed project — a QR code at the final walkthrough plus a text link — sustained at 10–15 a month. That pace crosses the measured leader's 44 inside three quarters, and it runs at zero cost.

E Built as a Referral Brochure + Land-Acquisition Funnel

The site was built as a referral brochure and a land-acquisition funnel — a legitimate job, just a different one than ranking in search. For a visitor who already has the name, it works: the story, the process, and the inventory are real assets.

In search, the domain ranks for 4 keywords, 0 in the top 10, at under one organic visit a month. The ~30 pages have no entry points for the 4,680 monthly buyer-intent searches in the Triangle.

The gap in one sentence: the site is assembled to serve the visitor who already has the name — and the Triangle produces ~4,680 buyer-intent searches a month it does not currently appear for.

F The Heading Layer Carries the Brand Story, Not the Query

H2s across the site are written as full marketing paragraphs, and the homepage carries 4 H1s. The heading layer is the first structure Google reads to decide what a page answers — a heading written as narrative describes the company; it does not claim a query. G The Foundation Is Sound — the Architecture Layer Was Never Added

Mechanically this is a healthy site: 93.4 on-page, clean HTTPS, zero layout shift. What's absent is structural, not mechanical:

  • 0 location pages — town-level searches across the Triangle have no page to resolve to.
  • 0 cost / comparison guides — the resource queries that feed both Google and AI search have no library here.
  • No schema beyond defaults — no GeneralContractor stack marking up the business, its services, or its projects for machine readers.
One note for the AI era: the site sits behind Cloudflare, whose default settings block AI crawlers. Worth unblocking regardless of the path chosen — AI answer engines can only cite what they can read.
