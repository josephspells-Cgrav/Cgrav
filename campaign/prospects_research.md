## Patterns across the Charlotte roofing market

1. **The outdated-site/great-work inversion is everywhere.** Nearly every 4.8+ rated family operator in this market has a website that looks 5–8 years behind their craftsmanship. The bullies (Masters, Waynco, Merritt, Race City, GHC) don't do better work — they present it better.
2. **Storm narrative is an unowned search lane.** Most prospects hide their storm/insurance-claim expertise behind a service-grid. The bullies dedicate entire landing pages to it. A storm-ready landing page is probably worth a single project on its own.
3. **Owner-story erasure is the most common flaw.** Father-son teams, husband-wife teams, 4-generation businesses, female GCs, USMC veterans, ex-insurance adjusters — every one of these is a hook a buyer would pay for, and almost none of them appear on the home hero.
4. **Gmail/Yahoo addresses on contractor websites are a scoring signal.** Philco, Full Spectrum both use free email — strong signal they've never hired a real website/brand partner.
5. **Concord + Mooresville are softer markets than Charlotte.** Fewer dominant bullies, smaller review counts needed to rank — easier wins on outskirts.
