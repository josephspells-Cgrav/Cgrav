## Patterns across the Charlotte roofing market

1. **Outdated-site / great-work inversion is everywhere.** Nearly every 4.8+ rated family operator has a website 5–8 years behind their craftsmanship. The bullies (Masters, Race City) don't do better work — they present it better.
2. **Storm narrative is an unowned search lane.** Most prospects hide storm/insurance-claim expertise behind a service grid. The bullies dedicate entire landing pages to it.
3. **Owner-story erasure is the most common flaw.** Father-son teams, husband-wife teams, 4-generation families, USMC and NFL veterans, ex-insurance adjusters — every hook is buried below the fold.
4. **Gmail/Yahoo addresses are a scoring signal.** Philco, Full Spectrum, Valhalla, Freedom RWS, All American Restoration all use free email — strong signal they've never hired a real website/brand partner.
5. **Direct-email findability is a quality filter.** All 20 kept here were publicly findable by direct email; the dropped prospects were not. Email-only outreach naturally selects for the more marketing-aware half of the market — which means easier pitches land on the other end.
6. **Brand-name payoff gap.** "Roof Wizard," "Blue Fox," "Thor," "Sentinels," "Mecklenburg," "Valhalla" — strong names used generically, with no visual or narrative follow-through on the domain itself.
