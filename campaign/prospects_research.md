## Patterns across the Charlotte roofing market

1. **The outdated-site/great-work inversion is everywhere.** Nearly every 4.8+ rated family operator in this market has a website that looks 5–8 years behind their craftsmanship. The bullies (Masters, Merritt, Race City, GHC) don't do better work — they present it better.
2. **Storm narrative is an unowned search lane.** Most prospects hide their storm/insurance-claim expertise behind a service-grid. The bullies dedicate entire landing pages to it.
3. **Owner-story erasure is the most common flaw.** Father-son teams, husband-wife teams, 4-generation businesses, USMC veterans, ex-insurance adjusters — every one of these is a hook a buyer would pay for, and almost none of them appear on the home hero.
4. **Gmail/Yahoo addresses are a scoring signal.** Philco and Full Spectrum both use free email — strong signal they've never hired a real website/brand partner.
5. **Direct-email findability is itself a quality filter.** The 10 prospects kept in this list were all findable by direct email; the 10 dropped were not. Email-only outreach naturally selects for the more marketing-aware half of the market — which means easier pitches land on the other end.
